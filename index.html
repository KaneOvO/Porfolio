Mutant

UCSC CMPM125 Final Project

Authors: Zexuan Li, Zhuo Chen, Guixiang Li, Lizhuoyuan Wan

Instructors: Tyler Coleman, Andrew Dunne, Ishaan Paranjape

Platform: Unity

Part I did: I Participated in the game brainstorming and planned the main gameplay, designed and wrote the interaction logic for all player-controllable characters, and planned the player's interaction with the levels together with the scene designer. Used Unity's animation state machine to create the animations for all characters in the game.

This was my final project for the UCSC CMPM125 course. In this project, our group's theme was 'fight fire with fire'. Based on this theme, we designed a system where players could transform into any enemy they had seen, and around this system, we created numerous level designs. Unfortunately, our initial plan was to add a final boss in the last scene, but due to insufficient development time, we had to abandon that idea. Through this project, I further strengthened the Unity development skills I learned in the 125 course. I also learned how to transform abstract design concepts into interactive game mechanics for players, deepening my understanding of programming and game design.

Eternal Dragon

UCSC CMPM120/ARTG120 Final Project

Authors: Zexuan Li, Zhuo Chen, Guixiang Li, Yuanxiao Wu

Instructors: Adam Smith, Zhaoqing Teng, Tam Cao, Patrick Michael Ballard, Angie Phan

Platform: Phaser

Part I did: I participated in the brainstorming for planning the game's gameplay, and wrote the entire storyline for the game. On the programming side, I was involved in creating the core logic framework of the game and also developed the game's second level.

This was my final project for the UCSC CMPM120 and ARTG120 course. In this project, our group's theme was 'Nearby in space, but distant in time'.Based on this theme, we wrote a story about time cycles. However, we were not satisfied with designing the gameplay as a traditional text adventure game, so we expanded the gameplay by displaying information through cards and interacting by dragging them. The feedback we received in the end was that this change was successful. Moreover, we designed multiple endings for the story and integrated the multiple-ending setting into our gameplay. The most impressive aspect of this project for me was that it was my first time taking on the role of a producer in a game development team. As such, I had to plan and oversee the project's progress, arrange work times for the team, and make decisions when the team faced disagreements. I encountered some difficulties in this role, but ultimately found ways to resolve them. This production experience was invaluable, especially in terms of teamwork. Now I feel more confident in team collaboration and am more willing to take responsibility within a group.

Farm simulation

UCSC CMPM121 Final Project

Authors: Zexuan Li, Zhuo Chen, Xing Zhong, Yuanxiao Wu

Instructors: Adam Smith, Nicholas Nolasco, Ryan Cai

Platform: Unity

Part I did: I was involved in brainstorming for player interaction logic and wrote the complete development documentation for the game. In terms of coding, the parts I primarily handled included player movement and pathfinding, planting mechanics, AoS byte array, internal DSL, and adaptation for the Android platform. Additionally, I contributed to modifying various components such as turn update logic, undo and redo functionalities, external DSL, both manual and automatic saving, and localization aspects of the game.

This was a farm simulation game where players could plant three different types of plants and needed to harvest a certain quantity within a specified date to pass the game. In this project, we prioritized design patterns over creativity. Besides implementing basic planting and harvesting, we also developed AoS byte arrays, undo and redo functions, manual and automatic saving, internal DSLs for defining plant growth conditions, external DSLs for setting game completion criteria, localization, and mobile platform adaptation, among other features. During this process, my team and I faced numerous challenges, but through discussion and repeated experimentation, we ultimately found solutions. By overcoming these challenges, I not only enhanced my technical skills and problem-solving abilities but also deepened my understanding of software engineering and design patterns.
